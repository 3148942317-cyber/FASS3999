Research Basis

Why this program matters

Drawn from philosophy, political science, international relations, and post-colonial theory — an interdisciplinary account of the structural drivers of Indigenous overrepresentation in Australia's criminal justice system.

Policy Context

Colonialism, Closing the Gap and education outcomes

First Nations people constitute approximately 3% of Australia's total population yet account for over 30% of the national prison population. First Nations women are imprisoned at more than 20 times the rate of non-Indigenous women.

A post-colonial lens reveals that Australia's carceral system operates as an extension of colonial governance — historically functioning to manage, surveil and contain Indigenous populations rather than deliver justice. The dispossession of land, the removal of children through successive assimilationist policies, and the erosion of cultural authority constitute a continuum of state violence that prefigures present-day overrepresentation.

The National Agreement on Closing the Gap (2020) targets educational parity between First Nations and non-Indigenous Australians. Outcome 6 aims for students to "achieve their full learning potential"; Outcome 5 targets Year 12 completion rates.

Sherbrooke-Smith & Carlton, 2018; Australian Bureau of Statistics, 2023.

Key finding — Royal Commission, 1991

"The most significant driver of Indigenous contact with the criminal justice system was their disadvantaged and unequal position in wider society."

Royal Commission into Aboriginal Deaths in Custody, 1991 (UNAA, 2021)

BOCSAR, 2024.

BOCSAR reports that Aboriginal people remain significantly overrepresented across all NSW criminal justice measures. Without upstream intervention, this pattern reproduces itself intergenerationally.

BOCSAR, 2024.

Year 12 Completion

In 2021, only 39% of First Nations Australians aged 20+ had completed Year 12 — up from 19.4% in 2001, but far short of the 70% tertiary qualification target set for 2031.

ABS, 2021; AIHW, 2025.

Government Investment

The Australian Government committed $20 million to the Australian Indigenous Education Foundation for scholarships, plus $71 million for remote boarding schools including a new Indigenous Education and Research Centre.

Department of Education, 2025.

Digital Access Gap

28% of regional NSW students have meaningful digital inclusion. The Rural Access Gap program improved device ratios from 1:6 to 1:4 — an important foundation for a digital component to this program.

WSU, 2024; Digital NSW, 2026.

Philosophical Grounding

The moral obligation to intervene

Rawlsian Justice

A Theory of Justice — John Rawls (2009)

Rawls holds that social and economic inequalities may only be permitted if they result in the bettering of the least advantaged. Applied to the overrepresentation of First Nations Australians in the criminal justice system — a direct result of systemic social and economic inequality — existing conditions fail this test. The disadvantaged are not advantaged by present inequalities; they are further constrained by them.

Rawls' concept of the "veil of ignorance" — designing systems without knowing one's place within them — reveals Australia's criminal justice system as one constructed without such impartiality. The existence of organisations addressing the injustices that result from this gap is itself evidence of systemic failure.

Rawls, J. (2009). A Theory of Justice. Harvard University Press.

Structural Theory

Structuration Theory — Giddens (1984)

Giddens holds that social structures are not external constraints imposed on individuals, but are reproduced through everyday institutional practices. In the context of incarceration, institutions including policing, courts, child protection and housing continually reproduce disadvantage for First Nations communities. The system does not simply reflect inequality — it perpetuates it.

When assessed against five power resources — political rights, official positions, coercive power, mobilisation power, and material power — Indigenous Australians are systematically disenfranchised across all five, leaving their capacity to reshape these structures largely out of reach.

Winter, 2009 (as cited in background research); Giddens, A. (1984). The Constitution of Society. Polity Press.

International Precedent

Global case studies

United States

Perry Preschool Project

Long-term longitudinal research on the Perry Preschool Project demonstrated the value of sustained investment at key developmental transition points — rather than only after harm has occurred. Early educational engagement produced measurable reductions in later justice-system contact, supporting the logic of upstream cultural and educational intervention.

Canada

Canadian Aboriginal Mentorship Program

This program demonstrated how long-term, culturally embedded mentorship from within communities reduces recidivism in ways that generic correctional programs do not. Community-grounded, identity-affirming support — the approach closest to this program's model — proved consistently more effective than institutional programs designed outside Indigenous contexts.
