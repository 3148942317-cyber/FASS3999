Program Design

Two-pronged cultural connection

A Year 7 cohort at Mian School, tracked through to graduation — combining face-to-face cultural immersion with a persistent digital platform.

Viewing as

For students

This program is built around you — your culture, your community, your future. Twice a year you will spend time with local Elders learning about your Country. Between those sessions, this website keeps everything connected.

For educators and Elders

Your leadership is central to this program. Sessions are shaped by local Elders and Aboriginal educators. The digital platform supports facilitation between face-to-face contact and provides access guidance for rural communities.

For government and partner organisations

This site is the proposal. The pilot targets one Year 7 cohort at Mian School, Dubbo, followed through to Year 12. The digital layer makes the model scalable to any rural or regional school without significant additional cost.

01

In-Person Cultural Immersion

Twice-yearly guided sessions with local Elders and Aboriginal educators. Sessions are shaped by local Country and community, not a generic national curriculum.

  • Local Country and place names
  • Language and oral tradition
  • Totems and Dreaming stories
  • Family histories and kinship structures
  • Cultural practices and ceremony
  • Local Indigenous success stories

02

Digital Support Platform

This website keeps cultural learning visible between sessions. It is accessible via school computers, libraries, and public spaces — designed for rural access realities.

  • Program timeline personalised by year level
  • Resource library linking cultural and support services
  • Moderated question box with Elder responses
  • Indigenous community testimonies
  • Educator and Elder facilitation guides
  • Access guidance for remote communities

Intended Impact

What success looks like

School Engagement

Reduced truancy, suspension and early school leaving among program participants compared to school-wide and state averages.

Cultural Identity

Students report a stronger sense of cultural belonging, connection to Country and relationship with community — measured through qualitative cohort check-ins.

Community Trust

Increased trust between students, school staff, Elders and families — evidenced through participation rates and educator feedback.

Scalable Model

The digital layer allows the model to be adapted for other rural and regional schools without full rebuilding — one platform, locally shaped content.

Note on measurement: Meaningful success is not captured by participation counts alone. Evaluation should centre student-reported wellbeing, Elder and community assessments of cultural depth, and long-term tracking of school completion and justice-system contact — conducted in partnership with the Mian School community, not externally imposed.
