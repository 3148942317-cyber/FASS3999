Program

Two-pronged cultural connection

The model starts with one Year 7 cohort at Mian School in Dubbo, then follows the cohort through graduation with recurring cultural sessions and online support.

1. In-Person Cultural Immersion

Students participate in guided sessions twice a year with local Elders and Aboriginal educators. The sessions focus on local Country, language, totems, Dreaming stories, family histories, cultural practices and local Indigenous success stories.

The pilot is designed for a Year 7 cohort and can continue with the same students through graduation.

2. Digital Support Platform

The website keeps cultural learning visible between sessions. It can hold a resource library, a program timeline, short testimonies, access guidance and a moderated question box.

Access can be supported through school computers, libraries and other trusted public spaces.

Program Elements

Local Knowledge

Learning is shaped by local Elders, local Country and the cultural context of the school community.

Resource Library

Students and educators can access trusted services, partner organisations and cultural learning materials.

Question Box

Questions are collected over time so students can keep engaging between face-to-face sessions.

Educator Support

Teachers and participating Elders can view guidance for access, facilitation and local adaptation.

Scalable Pilot

The model begins with one cohort, then can be adapted for other rural and regional schools.

Long-Term Impact

The goal is stronger cultural identity, student engagement, school trust and community connection.

Intended Impact

The program responds to the limits of reactive justice responses by building belonging and support earlier. It aims to reduce conditions that can contribute to youth offending and later incarceration.

Its value depends on local leadership, careful access planning, clear safeguarding and meaningful measures of engagement rather than superficial participation counts.
